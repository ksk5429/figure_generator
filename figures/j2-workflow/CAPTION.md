# j2-workflow

Computational workflow of the Op³ framework. Inputs (site properties,
foundation geometry, 11-scour sweep) feed Phase 1, where OptumGX runs
both load-deformation and limit analyses, producing depth-resolved
pressure/shear fields and per-scour VH failure envelopes. Phase 2 isolates
the first-harmonic component of the contact pressure, fits hyperbolic
p-y / t-z / Q-z backbones per 0.5 m depth slice, and constrains the
spring capacities against the VH envelope to give a globally-consistent
set of Winkler parameters. Phase 3 assembles the distributed BNWF model
in OpenSeesPy, attaches the required masses (soil plug, marine growth,
hydrodynamic added mass, rotor-nacelle assembly), applies stress
corrections for overburden loss below the scoured mudline, and computes
eigenvalues to produce f₁(S). The outputs are the scour-frequency curve
and the power-law fit |Δf/f₀| = 0.167·(S/D)^1.47. Dashed arrows indicate
the two validation loops: 70 g centrifuge tests (Kim 2025) and 32 months
of field monitoring at Gunsan (−3.9 % on baseline f₀).
