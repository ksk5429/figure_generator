# j3-bending-vs-tilting

Schematic illustration of the two distinct response modes of the
tripod suction-bucket foundation under progressive scour (manuscript
`@fig-bending-vs-tilting`, submitted Fig. 8). Two panels at the
190 mm column width.

**Panel (a) — Elastic bending mode.** The foundation remains intact;
all three buckets sit plumb with shallow symmetric scour around the
cluster. The tower bends elastically under lateral cyclic load — the
nacelle displacement _ū_ is moderate and the bottom bending strain
accumulates in proportion to the lost fixity-point depth. This is the
regime observed in T4 (dense saturated) at every tested _S_/_D_ and
in T5 (loose saturated) up to _S_/_D_ = 0.39.

**Panel (b) — Nonlinear tilting mode.** The upwind bucket yields
(schematically drawn as tilted and sunk); the whole foundation rotates
by the tilt angle _θ_ about that yielded bucket rather than bending as
a cantilever. The scour hole is drawn asymmetric — deeper on the
upwind side — consistent with the observed kinematic locking. Nacelle
displacement is amplified (heavy red arrow, "_ū_ amplified") while
bottom strain **reverses sign** because the tower carries its lateral
load through rigid-body rotation, not cantilever flexure. This is the
regime observed in T5 at _S_/_D_ = 0.58.

**What the dashed vertical plumb lines show.** Each panel carries a
dashed plumb line from the tower base upward. In (a) the actual tower
sits on the plumb line; in (b) the tower deviates by _θ_, which is
marked by a small red arc near mid-tower.

**Why this matters.** Frequency monitoring alone is nearly silent
across this transition (only 2.58 % drop at _S_/_D_ = 0.58 in T5). The
unmistakable signature is the combination of displacement
amplification (×4.27) and strain sign reversal captured in
`j3-mode-transition`. This schematic is the mechanistic story the
quantitative indicators bear out.
